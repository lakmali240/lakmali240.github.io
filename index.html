About Me

I’m a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Kentucky, specializing in Computer Vision and Medical Image Analysis. My research focuses on data-efficient learning for medical image segmentation — integrating Active Learning, foundation model adaptation, and mask-conditioned diffusion.

I have presented my work “A Warmer Start to Active Learning with Adaptive Gaussian Mixture Models for Skin Lesion Segmentation” at IEEE ICIP 2025, introducing a hybrid clustering-based active learning framework that addresses cold-start challenges.

I also bring industry research experience as a two-time Amazon Applied Scientist Intern — one focusing on Active Learning for Multi-Modal Classification and another on Generative Virtual Try-On with automated quality evaluation.
