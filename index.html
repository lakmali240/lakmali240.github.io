About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Kentucky, working in the Multimedia Information Analysis Laboratory (MIA Lab) under the supervision of Dr. Sen-ching Samson Cheung. My research lies at the intersection of Computer Vision, Medical Image Analysis, and Generative AI, with a focus on building efficient, label-aware learning systems for high-stakes visual domains.

My work spans three core themes:

  1. Active Learning & Annotation Efficiency: Designing self-supervised active learning frameworks that combine adaptive Gaussian Mixture Models with foundation models to achieve competitive medical image segmentation while significantly reducing annotation cost.
  2. Class-Imbalanced Segmentation: Developing difficulty-aware segmentation pipelines for gigapixel whole-slide histopathology images, enabling class-specific focal attention under severe class imbalance without auxiliary estimators.
  3. Generative AI for Product Visualization: Building end-to-end generative pipelines leveraging diffusion models, LoRA-based refinement, and LLM-driven quality evaluation for automated, context-aware product imagery at scale.

I have served as a two-time Amazon Applied Scientist Intern, developing production-scale multi-modal classification systems and generative AI pipelines for Merch on Demand. Prior to my PhD, I spent 3+ years as an Applications Engineer II at Synopsys, working on static verification solutions for RTL design.

I received my B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from the University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka (Washington Accord accredited program).
