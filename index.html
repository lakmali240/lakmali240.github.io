About me

I’m a PhD candidate in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Kentucky, focusing on computer vision and medical imaging. My recent work explores data-efficient segmentation through Active Learning, foundation-model adaptation (e.g., SAM-Path, UNI), and mask-conditioned diffusion for whole-slide pathology and dermoscopy.

Highlights include an ICIP 2025 paper on Adaptive GMM-based early-stage selection (TAIAL), and two Amazon Applied Scientist internships: (1) Active Learning for multi-modal, multi-class theme recognition; and (2) a Generative Virtual Try-On pipeline with demographic-aware model selection, a garment-fidelity refiner, and a fully automated quality evaluation system. I enjoy building robust training on DGX/HPC and collaborating on problems where smarter data reduces labeling costs.
